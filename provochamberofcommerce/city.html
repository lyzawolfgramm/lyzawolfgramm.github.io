PROVO CITYS HISTORY

Provo was discovered in 1849 by pioneers who were members of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints. It was originally named Timpanogas meaning "rock river" but was later renamed Provo in 1850. Provo is the second largest metropolitan area in the state of Utah and has a population of over 116,594 people. The average age in Provo is 23.6 years and 91,000 of the people in Provo are made up of adults.



HOME OF BRIGHAM YOUNG UNIVERSITY

Brigham Young University is in the heart of Provo. Over 40,000 of the population in Provo are enrolled college students at BYU. Provo is has also been ranked as the 2nd greatest place to live in America along with 2nd for best work life balance, 3rd for business and career, 2nd for fastest growing city, 9th highest cycling rate in the nation and 1st fastest growing on the rise start up hubs!
